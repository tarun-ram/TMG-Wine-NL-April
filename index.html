Dear Wine Cellar Member,

If you’re planning a street party for the King’s Coronation, you’ll have been cheered by predictions this week from weather experts that the long-range forecast for Saturday 6 May is set to buck the current trend of ‘miserable’ and ‘more miserable’. Trudging to work this morning in my waterproof coat, a scarf wrapped round my neck, I struggled to imagine temperatures reaching double figures, let alone a balmy 24°C. On the off-chance that the meteorologists aren’t entirely accurate, I intend to bring the bank holiday sunshine in vinous form. We’ve put together a case of wines that will suit the celebratory mood – and transport you and your guests to warmer climes. Among the selection, you’ll find a zesty vinho verde, a top-notch pink prosecco and a banker of a Chilean cab. Don't forget that as a new Wine Cellar member you're entitled to £10 off your first order over £50. Click here to shop our range of mixed cases, including the Street Party Selection.

This week, we reveal the best wines from the Co-op; Matt Walls uncovers the seven white varieties you need to know; and we get up-close with merlot, a grape that is undeserving of its sometimes lukewarm reputation.

Cheers,

Amber Dalton, Editor

Editor’s pick of the week

Quinta da Lixa QL Loureiro 2021, Vinho Verde, Portugal
Charles and Camilla released the recipe for their Coronation quiche last week. The inclusion of tarragon may have divided opinion; it also presents a challenge when it comes to wine matching. Victoria Moore recommends this gently floral vinho verde: tangy and refreshing with a citrussy backbone it’s an easy-drinker that just needs a bit of sunshine. Lovely label, too.
